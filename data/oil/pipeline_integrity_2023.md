# Pipeline Integrity Assessment Report 2023
## Gulf Coast Operations — Texas and Louisiana Segments

### Executive Summary
This report covers the annual integrity assessment for 847 miles of mainline pipeline across Texas and Louisiana. Three segments show elevated concern based on inline inspection data collected Q2 2023.

### Segment A — Eastern Corridor (miles 120–230)
- ILI run completed March 2023
- 14 metal loss anomalies detected exceeding 50% wall thickness
- Subsidence rate: 0.8 inches/year over 3-year trend
- Recommended: pressure reduction pending hydrotest

### Segment B — Coastal Crossing (miles 445–512)
- Last ILI: 2019 — reinspection overdue per PHMSA schedule
- Hurricane-related scour suspected at 3 crossings (Katrina track overlap)
- Visual inspection inconclusive — diving survey scheduled Q4 2023

### Segment C — Refinery Approach (miles 790–847)
- Cathodic protection system functioning within spec
- External corrosion detected at 2 road crossings (coating holiday)
- Repair completed July 2023

### Regulatory Status
- 6 open PHMSA conditions from 2022 inspection cycle
- 2 conditions delinquent past 90-day resolution target
- Next scheduled audit: February 2024

### Recommendations
1. Expedite ILI on Segment B — risk of uncharacterised corrosion
2. Address delinquent PHMSA conditions by Q4 2023
3. Implement leak detection upgrade on eastern corridor
