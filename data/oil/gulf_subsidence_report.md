# Gulf Coast Land Subsidence Report
## Infrastructure Risk Assessment — Pipeline Operations

### Background
Land subsidence in the Gulf Coast region presents a significant and ongoing hazard to buried pipeline infrastructure. This report synthesises data from USGS, Harris County Flood Control District, and operator survey records.

### Subsidence Rates by Zone

| Zone | Rate (in/yr) | Pipeline Exposure | Last Survey |
|------|-------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Houston Ship Channel | 1.2–2.1 | 34 miles | 2022 |
| Bayou Corridor | 0.6–1.4 | 18 miles | 2021 |
| Eastern Offshore Approach | 0.3–0.9 | 22 miles | 2019 |
| Coastal Wetland Segments | 0.8–1.8 | 41 miles | 2020 |

### Key Findings
1. Eastern Offshore Approach — no survey data post-2019. Subsidence modelling indicates 2.4–4.8 inches of cumulative movement unaccounted for in current stress calculations.
2. Three pipeline segments in Houston Ship Channel zone show differential movement consistent with bell-hole corrosion acceleration.
3. Coastal Wetland Segments: subsidence combined with storm surge cycles creates repeated inundation that degrades coating integrity faster than design assumptions.

### Data Gaps
- No GPS/InSAR integration for real-time subsidence monitoring
- Eastern Offshore Approach survey 4 years out of date
- Post-2022 seismic cluster (magnitude 2.4–3.6) in Brazoria County not factored into current pipeline stress models

### Financial Exposure
Based on historical Gulf Coast pipeline incidents, uncharacterised subsidence exposure of this magnitude correlates with:
- Repair cost range: $4.2M–$67M depending on failure location and response time
- Third-party liability: additional 2–5x repair cost for spill events
