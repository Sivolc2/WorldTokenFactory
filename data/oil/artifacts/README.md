## Scenario B — Permian Basin "Assumed Backstop" (West Texas)

The Permian produces ~45% of US oil (~6.7 Mbbl/day) and is being treated as the global relief valve for a Hormuz crisis. Three underreported structural vulnerabilities make this assumption dangerous: ERCOT grid failure risk, zombie well / wastewater pressure buildup, and Waha gas pipeline bottleneck (Blackcomb delayed to Nov 2026).

See `../permian_basin_risk_brief.md` for full scenario analysis.

### `permian_basin_midland_dem_30m.tif` (34MB)
- **Source**: Copernicus GLO-30 Digital Surface Model (TanDEM-X, 30m)
- **Coverage**: N31–32, W102–103 — Delaware Basin / western Permian, West Texas
- **Why**: Shows the flat basin terrain crossed by dense pipeline and well infrastructure. The flatness that makes it look stable is deceptive — subsurface wastewater injection pressure is invisible at surface.
- **License**: Public domain, Copernicus Open Data

### `texas_wastewater_injection_seismicity_azle_natcomms2015.pdf` (1.1MB)
- **Source**: Nature Communications (2015) — Hornbach et al., DOI: 10.1038/ncomms7728
- **Title**: "Causal factors for seismicity near Azle, Texas"
- **Why**: Open-access study demonstrating that combined brine extraction + wastewater injection triggered earthquakes along a Texas fault system — the exact mechanism for Risk Vector 2. Study site is Barnett Shale / Fort Worth, but the causal mechanism and regulatory implications directly apply to Permian Basin Class II injection wells.
- **License**: Open access, Nature Communications

### `eia_drilling_productivity_report_permian.pdf` (701KB)
- **Source**: U.S. Energy Information Administration — Monthly Drilling Productivity Report
- **Why**: This is THE document energy analysts use to project Permian Basin production. It shows the production assumptions the world is depending on — the "backstop" that is quietly cracking. Public domain government report.
- **License**: Public domain, US Government

### `permian_power_grid_risk.mkv` (51MB)
- **Source**: YouTube, `youtube.com/watch?v=rrktPzN06nA`
- **Title**: "Permian Basin Power Shortage: How Oil & Gas Operators Can Overcome Grid Constraints"
- **Duration**: ~23 min — covers ERCOT dependency, compressor/pump failure risk, and infrastructure gap
- **Why**: Directly addresses Risk Vector 1 (ERCOT grid failure → field operation curtailment). Industry experts discussing the exact vulnerability described in the risk brief.
- **License**: Fair use for demo/research purposes
