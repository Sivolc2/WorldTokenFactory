# Site: Permian Fields
**Coordinates**: 31.47°N, 103.74°W — Reeves/Loving County border, Delaware Basin, West Texas

## What's here
One of the densest active well pad clusters in the United States. This is the core of the
"assumed backstop" scenario — the production area the world expects to ramp output during
a Hormuz crisis, but which faces ERCOT grid failure risk, wastewater pressure buildup, and
Waha gas egress constraints.

## Files
- `dem_30m.tif` (34MB) — Copernicus GLO-30 DEM, N31 W104 tile. Flat basin terrain; the
  flatness is deceptive — subsurface pressure is invisible at surface.
- `optical_modis_truecolor.tif` (1.5MB) — NASA GIBS MODIS TrueColor, 2024-06-15, 250m.
  bbox: 31.2–31.7°N, 103.4–104.1°W
