# Site: GOM Offshore Buffer — Thunder Horse PDQ
**Coordinates**: 28.17°N, 88.49°W — Mississippi Canyon Block 778/822, Gulf of Mexico
~150 miles southeast of New Orleans, ~6,050 ft water depth

## What's here
Thunder Horse PDQ (Production, Drilling and Quarters) — a BP semi-submersible, one of
the largest deepwater platforms in the Gulf of Mexico. Produces ~60–80 Kbbl/day. The GOM
deepwater fleet serves as a secondary supply buffer when Permian is constrained.

No Copernicus DEM available (all ocean). Replaced with GEBCO bathymetry showing the
Mississippi Canyon — a dramatic submarine canyon that drops from ~200m shelf to >3,000m
within the tile. The canyon is why this block has such deep water and why it requires a
semi-submersible rather than a fixed platform.
