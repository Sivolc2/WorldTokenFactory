# Financial Loss Modelling Guide
## For Risk Factor Analysis

### Methodology
Loss ranges are estimated using a three-component model:
1. **Direct loss**: repair/remediation cost at point of failure
2. **Consequential loss**: downstream production impact, supply chain disruption
3. **Liability exposure**: third-party claims, regulatory penalties

### Industry Benchmarks

#### Oil and Gas Infrastructure
- Pipeline rupture (onshore): $2M–$150M depending on diameter, product, location
- Platform blowout: $50M–$5B (Macondo as upper bound)
- Regulatory penalty (PHMSA): $10K–$1M per violation, up to $10M per incident

#### Agricultural / Specialty Farming
- Disease outbreak (full flock): 60–100% of annual production value
- Regulatory seizure: compliance cost $50K–$500K plus lost revenue
- Predator event: 5–25% of population, $20K–$200K replacement cost

### Uncertainty Premium
When uncertainty is high, apply a multiplier to the base loss range:
- High uncertainty (>0.7): 3–5x base range
- Medium uncertainty (0.4–0.7): 1.5–2x base range
- Low uncertainty (<0.4): base range appropriate

### Token Investment Curve
Typical return on analysis investment:
- 1,000 tokens: 15–25% uncertainty reduction
- 5,000 tokens: 35–45% uncertainty reduction
- 20,000 tokens: 55–65% uncertainty reduction
- Primary data collection: 70–85% uncertainty reduction possible
