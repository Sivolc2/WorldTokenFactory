# Lemming Farmers Demo — Real Artifacts

## Scenario
Humorous but demonstrative: Norwegian lemming population risk. Lemmings (Lemmus lemmus) undergo violent 3–4 year boom-bust population cycles that are still not fully understood — a perfect example of irreducible ecological uncertainty. Farmers and land managers on the Hardangervidda plateau are directly affected by these cycles.

**The cliff angle**: The GeoTIFF covers Hardangervidda National Park — Norway's prime lemming habitat — which has a dramatic western escarpment dropping toward Hardangerfjord. The plateau-to-cliff terrain is literally where the lemming cliff myth originates. The Disney "White Wilderness" (1958) staged lemmings being herded off cliffs in Alberta; actual Norwegian lemmings do fall into rivers and fjords during migrations, but the uncertainty is *when and why* the population collapses, not whether they're suicidal.

## Files

### `hardangervidda_lemming_habitat_dem_30m.tif` (19MB)
- **Source**: Copernicus GLO-30 Digital Surface Model (TanDEM-X, 30m resolution)
- **Coverage**: N60–61, E7–8 — Hardangervidda National Park, Norway
- **Why**: Shows the high plateau (~1,140m) with the dramatic western cliff-drop to Hardangerfjord. Prime Norwegian lemming habitat. The terrain perfectly illustrates the "cliff" metaphor.
- **License**: Public domain, Copernicus Open Data

### `lemming_population_cycles_hardangervidda.pdf` (628KB)
- **Source**: Scientific Reports, Selås (2016) — DOI: 10.1038/srep27225
- **Title**: "Timing of population peaks of Norway lemming in relation to atmospheric pressure"
- **Why**: Quantitative analysis of 3–4 year boom-bust cycles across Hardangervidda and other Norwegian sites. Statistical models show high spatial synchrony over 1,000 km but still-uncertain causal mechanisms. Directly demonstrates compounding ecological uncertainty.
- **License**: CC BY 4.0
