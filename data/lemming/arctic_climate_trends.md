# Arctic Climate Trends: Impact on Small Mammal Farming
## Synthesis Report for Lemming Husbandry Operations

### Temperature Trends — Dovrefjell Region
- Mean winter temperature increase: +2.1°C over 30-year baseline
- Freeze-thaw cycles per winter: increased from 4 to 11 (2000–2023 trend)
- Snowpack duration shortened by 23 days since 1990
- Permafrost active layer: deepening at 3.2cm/year

### Population Cycle Disruption
Norwegian lemming populations naturally cycle on 3–5 year boom-bust patterns. Climate disruption is affecting cycle predictability:
- 2018–2023: 3 consecutive suppressed peak years (historically rare)
- Population synchrony across regions breaking down
- Modelling suggests continued warming will produce higher variance, less predictable cycles

### Implications for Farming Operations
1. **Breeding stock reliability**: Wild population crashes reduce genetic diversity available for farm replenishment
2. **Hibernation disruption**: Warmer winters mean lemmings do not fully hibernate, increasing metabolic stress
3. **Lichen regeneration**: Warmer, wetter conditions favour competing moss species over lichen, reducing natural food availability
4. **Infrastructure**: Permafrost thaw undermining enclosure foundations is a 10–20 year structural risk

### Risk Assessment
High risk of yield disruption in any given year due to climate-induced population cycle variability. Recommend captive breeding programme independent of wild stock by 2026.
