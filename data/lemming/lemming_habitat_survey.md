# Norwegian Lemming Habitat Survey
## Enclosure Suitability Assessment — Dovrefjell Region

### Overview
This survey assesses the suitability of current enclosure infrastructure for sustainable lemming farming operations. Survey conducted August 2023, covering 14 active enclosures and 3 proposed expansion sites.

### Current Enclosure Status

| Enclosure | Size (m²) | Population | Condition | Cliff Access |

| C-1 | 800 | 102 | Good | Partial — mesh damage |

### Key Findings
1. **Cliff Access (B-1)**: Northern boundary lacks adequate fencing. Observed 3 individuals approaching cliff edge during survey. Immediate remediation required.
2. **Permafrost Integrity**: Two enclosures showing signs of active layer deepening. Foundation movement detected at B-2 corner posts.
3. **Predator Evidence**: Snowy owl perch marks observed near A-2 and C-1. One arctic fox burrow within 200m of B-2.
4. **Vegetation**: Lichen coverage declining in A-1 and A-2 due to overgrazing. Supplemental feed dependency increasing.

### Population Density Risk
Current average density of 8.3 lemmings per 100m² exceeds recommended maximum of 6.0 for stress-free husbandry. B-1 at 10.8/100m² is a disease amplification risk.

### Climate Observations
Snowpack depth in winter 2022–23 was 40% below historical average, disrupting natural hibernation cycles. 12% of monitored individuals showed abnormal activity during January–February window.
